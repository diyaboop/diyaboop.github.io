Background & Problem

Platforms like LinkedIn were built for a different era of hiring — one where resumes, credentials, and professional networks determined access to opportunity. In 2025, that model is broken for early-career talent. Students can't stand out in a sea of identical applications, and hiring managers can't identify genuinely qualified candidates with confidence.

Student Problem

Students need a way to stand out in the job application process to access meaningful career opportunities — beyond the resume and GPA.

Hiring Manager Problem

Hiring managers need a way to identify and engage qualified early-career talent to make confident hiring decisions — without wading through hundreds of indistinguishable profiles.

The core tension: Existing platforms optimize for volume and credentials, not demonstrated ability or potential fit. The missing layer is a mechanism for proving skills and building trust — on both sides of the hiring relationship.

Research & Discovery

We conducted 40+ user interviews across students, hiring managers, and university career center staff — using empathy mapping, stakeholder mapping, and journey mapping to uncover the structural roots of the problem.

Key Research Insights

  • Students feel invisible — applying cold to job postings produces near-zero signal. Credentials don't differentiate.
  • Hiring managers distrust unverified skills — they want evidence of capability, not self-reported proficiency.
  • Networking is broken — existing platforms make it hard to build authentic connections; transactional referral dynamics dominate.
  • Trust is the missing ingredient — both sides want a trusted intermediary that can vouch for ability and intent.
  • Gen Z demands meaning — students are drawn to purpose-driven career pathways, not just salary optimization.

PEST Context

  • Economic: Recession fears stalling entry-level hiring, making differentiation even more critical
  • Technological: Advanced AI now easier to implement — enabling smarter matching at scale
  • Legal: Data privacy laws tightening globally — any solution must be transparent by design
  • Social: Gen Z prioritizes purpose and growth over prestige

Solutions Designed

Using the SCAMPER ideation framework and multiple prototype-and-test cycles, we converged on two primary product solutions and a supporting feature — each targeting a different layer of the trust and discovery problem.

Primary · "No Brainer"
Skill Bill 2.0

A skill assessment platform designed to be casual, low-pressure, and credential-independent. Candidates choose from different types of skill tests based on their strengths, track progress, and surface real-world problem-solving ability — not just what's on a resume.

  • Reduces candidate pressure vs. traditional assessments
  • Generates audit-ready skill evidence for hiring managers
  • Candidates choose test formats matching their strengths
Primary · "Utility"
Networkeering

A platform combining networking, volunteering, and mentorship with a points-based reputation system. Encourages active participation, builds authentic connections, and makes trustworthiness measurable rather than assumed.

  • Points system rewards genuine engagement, not vanity metrics
  • Bridges borderless mentorship across access gaps
  • Non-transactional referral model — purpose over perks
Supporting feature — Mentorship Engine: Smart mentorship matching to ensure meaningful mentor-mentee connections. Avoids impersonal cold application links in favor of direct, open, non-transactional connections. The goal is human signal, not automated matching noise.
