Problem

Fraud detection models flag millions of transactions daily. When false positives concentrate on specific customer profiles — credit card holders, certain product categories, non-US billing addresses — the system creates measurable disparate impact without any discriminatory intent.

Most fraud teams monitor aggregate precision and recall. They do not segment performance by customer group. Bias audits, when they happen at all, are manual, infrequent, and assembled under pressure before regulatory exams. There is no automated alerting when segment-level disparity exceeds acceptable thresholds.

The invisible harm: A model can achieve 92% recall globally while systematically wronging a specific customer segment at 2–3× the rate of others. Aggregate metrics hide this. FraudWatch makes it visible — and quantifies whether the disparity is statistically real or random noise.

Solution

FraudWatch is an end-to-end ML pipeline that trains a fraud classifier, audits it for demographic bias using z-tests for statistical significance, and monitors performance drift weekly — all queryable through a live FastAPI layer and stored in SQLite for full audit traceability.

Regulatory Context

FraudWatch is designed so that compliance documentation is a byproduct of normal operations — not a pre-audit scramble. Every audit result is timestamped and stored in SQLite. Bias findings map to specific regulatory requirements.

ECOA
Prohibits credit discrimination. FPR disparity detection by segment proxy quantifies disparate impact before it reaches regulators.
EU AI Act · Art. 10–13
Bias testing required for high-risk AI in credit scoring. Automated audit with statistical evidence and human oversight documentation.
CFPB UDAAP
False positive burden constitutes potential consumer harm. FraudWatch makes that harm measurable and attributable to specific segments.
SR 11-7
Model risk management requires validation and ongoing monitoring. Weekly drift tracking with configurable alerts and full audit trail satisfies this.

Who This Is Built For

Risk Analyst

Needs segment-level FPR by card type and product category to identify which customer groups are being disproportionately blocked — and a z-test to confirm the disparity is real before escalating to leadership.

Compliance Officer

Needs exportable, structured audit reports with regulatory mapping — ready to include in model risk documentation without manual compilation. FraudWatch generates these as CSV exports from every audit run.

ML Engineer

Needs automated alerts when weekly recall drops below threshold, and week-by-week metrics to identify when drift started and correlate it with data or behavioral changes.

Product Manager

Needs a single dashboard showing recall, precision, and FPR trends to communicate model health to leadership in a weekly review — without a data science handoff every time.

Business Impact

  • 45,375 false positives surfaced in a single test window — each a wrongly blocked customer transaction that would otherwise go undetected
  • Zero drift across 12 weeks confirms model stability and enables confident production deployment without manual monitoring overhead
  • Full pipeline reproducible with one command — reducing audit prep time from days to minutes
  • Compliance documentation generated continuously, not assembled manually before audits
  • Quantifies regulatory exposure under ECOA, EU AI Act, CFPB UDAAP, and SR 11-7 — before an examiner does
The broader point: A fraud model that achieves 92% recall globally can still be systematically harming a specific customer segment. FraudWatch operationalizes the insight that responsible AI isn't a checkbox — it requires continuous, automated, statistically rigorous auditing built into the deployment lifecycle.
